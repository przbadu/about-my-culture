NEPAL

Land of mystic mountains and Rich in cultural heritages, Nepal is a paradise for hikers hidden between China and India.

National Flag

  • The blue border symbolizes peace and harmoney
  • The crimson red is Nepal's national colour, and reflects the brave spirit of the Nepalese people.
  • The two triangles symbolize the Himalayan Mountains
  • The sun and the moon represents permanence
  • The national flag of Nepal is the world's only non-quadrilateral flag that acts as both the state flag and civil flag of a sovereign country

Independent Country

  • Ended with Sugauli Treaty in which One third part of nepal was taken, as per treaty, and now they are part of india.

Gurkhas - Great Warriors

  • Gorkhali, Nepali a.k.a Gurkhas
  • "Better to die than be a coward" is the motto of the world-famous Nepalese Gurkha soldiers who are an integral part of the British Army.
  • The name "Gurkha" comes from the hill town of Gorkha from which the Nepalese kingdom had expanded.
  • Weapon: Khukuri
  • After suffering heavy casualties in the invasion of Nepal, the British East India Company signed a hasty peace deal in 1815, which also allowed it to recruit from the ranks of the former enemy.
  • Gurkhas never leave a man behind
  • The Gurkhas have loyally fought for the British all over the world, receiving 13 Victoria Crosses and thousands of other medals in recognition of extraordinary acts of bravery.
  • The selection process has been described as one of the toughest in the world and is fiercely contested. Young hopefuls have to run uphill for 40 minutes carrying a wicker basket on their back filled with rocks weighing 70lbs.
